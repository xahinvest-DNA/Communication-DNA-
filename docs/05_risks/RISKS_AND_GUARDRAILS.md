# RISKS AND GUARDRAILS

## Key product risks

### 1. False certainty
The system may appear to "know" another person’s inner world.
Guardrail: always frame deeper readings as hypotheses with confidence and evidence signals.

### 2. Validation addiction
The user may use the product to feed obsessive interpretation loops.
Guardrail: return the user to position, reality, and action rather than infinite decoding.

### 3. Manipulation support
The user may seek better tools for guilt, coercion, or control.
Guardrail: avoid optimization toward domination and emotional extraction.

### 4. Dependency
The user may stop trusting their own judgment.
Guardrail: design for learning and agency, not total outsourcing.

### 5. Over-pathologizing
The product may label normal ambiguity as deep dysfunction.
Guardrail: stay close to textual evidence and relational dynamics rather than pseudo-clinical claims.

### 6. Privacy failure
Users may bring highly sensitive content.
Guardrail: privacy must later become a first-order design principle, not a legal afterthought.
