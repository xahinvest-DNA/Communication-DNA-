# DECODER_MIRROR_NAVIGATOR

## Core internal model
Personal mode can be understood as three connected internal subsystems.

## 1. Decoder
Purpose:
- read the external communication material;
- identify observable signals;
- estimate likely hidden dynamics;
- separate signal from story.

Questions:
- what is actually present in the text;
- what hypotheses does it support;
- what remains uncertain.

## 2. Mirror
Purpose:
- reflect what is happening inside the user;
- identify hidden need, reactive pattern, or false aim;
- detect where the user is not reading only the text but also their own unresolved narrative.

Questions:
- what is the user seeking beneath the explicit question;
- what makes this contact so charged;
- what kind of projection risk is active.

## 3. Navigator
Purpose:
- turn reading into position and possible action;
- identify what not to do;
- generate grounded next moves.

Questions:
- what is the user’s actual position;
- what moves are available;
- what is the cost of each move;
- which move best aligns with reality and user aim.

## Product implication
A weak product skips directly from input to advice.
A strong product moves through:
- Decoder;
- Mirror;
- Navigator.

## Working formula
Decoder -> Mirror -> Navigator
