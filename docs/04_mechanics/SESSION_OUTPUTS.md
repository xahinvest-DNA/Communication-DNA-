# SESSION OUTPUTS

## Candidate output blocks
A session output may later include:
1. short factual reading of the exchange;
2. likely hidden need of the other side;
3. likely hidden need of the user;
4. interpretation risks;
5. dynamic pattern name;
6. confidence level;
7. possible next moves;
8. one recommended move;
9. caution note.

## Why this matters
The product should not stop at interpretation. It should help convert analysis into a cleaner decision or action.

## Constraint
Outputs should remain readable under emotional load. The user is often flooded and cannot absorb overly dense analysis.
