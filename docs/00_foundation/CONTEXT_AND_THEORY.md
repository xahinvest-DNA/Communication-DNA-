# CONTEXT AND THEORY

## Main theoretical framing
This product should be understood not as a generic "chat analyzer", but as a system for navigating hidden communication structure.

A user does not bring only text. The user brings a knot of uncertainty:
- what is happening here;
- what does the other person want;
- what do I want;
- where am I projecting;
- where is the loss of contact;
- what move changes the dynamic.

## Working assumption
Most communication pain does not come only from words themselves. It comes from the interaction between:
1. the literal text;
2. the structure of the relationship;
3. the user’s interpretation;
4. the user’s hidden need;
5. the user’s habitual reaction pattern.

## Product hypothesis
The product becomes stronger when it does not merely explain the other person, but also:
- reflects the user’s own state and bias;
- separates signal from projection;
- identifies repeating scenarios;
- proposes a next move from clarity rather than impulse.

## Important distinction
The system should avoid pretending certainty about another person’s mind.
Preferred framing:
- likely motive;
- possible hidden need;
- confidence level;
- observable signals in the text;
- alternative interpretations.

## Deep value
The long-term value is not only immediate advice.
The deeper value is teaching the user to:
- read communication more accurately;
- reduce fantasy overlays;
- detect manipulation and false hope;
- see their own repetitive patterns;
- respond from position instead of reactivity.

## Personal mode as broad territory
Personal mode includes, but is not limited to:
- romance;
- friendship;
- family;
- ex-relationships;
- partnership with emotional entanglement;
- self-dialogue;
- difficult decisions tied to identity and attachment.

## Theory preservation rule
At this stage, theory must remain wider than product packaging. We should preserve nuanced observations even if they do not fit the first MVP.
