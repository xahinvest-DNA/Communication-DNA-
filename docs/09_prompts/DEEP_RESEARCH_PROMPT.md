# DEEP RESEARCH PROMPT

Conduct deep product and behavioral research for a theory-first product called Communication DNA in personal mode.

The product analyzes human text communication and helps users understand:
- likely hidden needs and motives;
- communication structure;
- interpretation risks;
- repeating patterns;
- possible next moves.

Research goals:
1. identify the strongest real-life user scenarios;
2. map explicit and hidden jobs-to-be-done;
3. identify competing products and adjacent substitutes;
4. clarify trust, privacy, and ethical barriers;
5. compare candidate product forms;
6. identify which wedge could be the strongest MVP entry point.

The research should distinguish between:
- what users say they want;
- what they repeatedly seek under uncertainty;
- what actually changes their behavior after analysis.

Output structure:
- market patterns;
- scenario clusters;
- user motivations;
- trust barriers;
- product opportunities;
- risk map;
- recommendations for the next theory step.
